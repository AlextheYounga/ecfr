##### § 500.18 SI metric prefixes. #####

The following chart indicates SI prefixes that may be used on a broad range of consumer commodity labels:

|                                             Prefix                                             |Symbol|Multiplying factor <sup>1</sup>|
|------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|------|-------------------------------|
|                                             Kilo-                                              |  k   |       x 10<sup>3</sup>        |
|                                             Deca-                                              |  da  |             × 10              |
|                                             Deci-                                              |  d   |       × 10<sup>−1</sup>       |
|                                             Centi-                                             |  c   |       × 10<sup>−2</sup>       |
|                                             Milli-                                             |  m   |       × 10<sup>−3</sup>       |
|                                             Micro-                                             |  µ   |       × 10<sup>−6</sup>       |
|<sup>1</sup> 10<sup>2</sup>=100; 10<sup>3</sup>=1000; 10<sup>−1</sup>=0.1; 10<sup>−2</sup>=0.01.|      |                               |
|                   Thus, 2 kg=2 × 1000 g=2000 g, and 3 cm=3 × 0.01 m=0.03 m.                    |      |                               |
